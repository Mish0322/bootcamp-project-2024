Diving into "The Only Woman in the Room" by Marie Benedict

This book outlines the real story of Hedy Lamarr's journey from an actress to an inventor. Benedict illustrates her life in Austria, her escape from her controlling husband who was involved with Hitler, and her contributions to World War II. The struggles of women is pictured throughout her life by showing how she was only valued for her beauty although her brains were strong too. Without Lamarr's invention, we may not have today's technology of Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. Unfortunately, not many people know she was the brains behind this as she was a woman and the world did not want to recognize her achievement. This book is truly captivating and makes you understand the challenges of women and the harsh times of World War II.
